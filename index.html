QA Test Engineer

March 2018 - February 2019

HydroPoint Data Systems

Petaluma, CA


I was mostly a manual tester, but I did do some automation with Selenium and Python in Pycharm. At HydroPoint I:

  • Designed and implemented black box, sanity, smoke, and regression test strategies,
  • Developed deep expertise in company products (3 web applications and multiple firmware versions),
  • Documented test cases with the TestRail test case management application,
  • Thoroughly explained and documented defects via the Jira issue tracking system,
  • Performed pre-release preparations and post-release sanity testing,
  • Collaborated with the broader engineering team to identify, understand, and resolve the root causes of defects and customer escalations, and
  • Configured and maintained up to 4 test environments.

During the second half of my time at HydroPoint, I found that I was having a hard time prioritizing my tasks and figuring out how to relate to my boss, and I upset a coworker due to this. However, I learned a lot, like that my manager isn't going to roll their eyes at me because I asked what they want me to finish first, and that if I'm having trouble with getting things done, I can ask my manager for tips and let him know I'm struggling. This lesson is something I want to keep in mind at my next job.

Additionally, automated testing at HydroPoint was rather difficult for me, and I learned (after a lot of Googling) that it was all due to asychronous data and not my code. I didn't get very far on learning to how poll a site for results, but I plan on doing so before May comes.
